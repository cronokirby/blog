Topological Groups are Hausdorff

A Topological Group is a Group Object in the Category Top\bold{Top}Top of Topological Spaces and Continuous Functions.

Concretely, this is a topological space GGG, endowed with two continuous functions:

An extra assumption, made at least by Munkres, is that GGG is a T1T_1T1​ space. This means that for any points x,y∈G,x≠yx, y \in G, x \neq yx,y∈G,x=y, we have neighborhoods x∈Ux,y∈Uyx \in U_x, y \in U_yx∈Ux​,y∈Uy​ that don't contain the other point, i.e. x∉Uy,y∉Uxx \notin U_y, y \notin U_xx∈/Uy​,y∈/Ux​.
