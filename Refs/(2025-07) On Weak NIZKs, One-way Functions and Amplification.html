Abstract

An (ϵs,ϵzk)(\epsilon_\mathsf{s},\epsilon_{\mathsf{zk}})(ϵs​,ϵzk​)-weak non-interactive zero knowledge (NIZK) argument has soundness error at most ϵs\epsilon_\mathsf{s}ϵs​ and zero-knowledge error at most ϵzk\epsilon_{\mathsf{zk}}ϵzk​. We show that as long as NP\mathsf{NP}NP is hard in the worst case, the existence of an (ϵs,ϵzk)(\epsilon_\mathsf{s}, \epsilon_{\mathsf{zk}})(ϵs​,ϵzk​)-weak NIZK proof or argument for NP\mathsf{NP}NP with ϵzk+ϵs<1\epsilon_{\mathsf{zk}} + \sqrt{\epsilon_\mathsf{s}} < 1ϵzk​+ϵs​​<1 implies the existence of one-way functions. To obtain this result, we introduce and analyze a strong version of universal approximation that may be of independent interest.

As an application, we obtain NIZK amplification theorems based on very mild worst-case complexity assumptions. Specifically, [Bitansky-Geier, CRYPTO'24] showed that (ϵs,ϵzk)(\epsilon_\mathsf{s}, \epsilon_{\mathsf{zk}})(ϵs​,ϵzk​)-weak NIZK proofs (with ϵs\epsilon_\mathsf{s}ϵs​ and ϵzk\epsilon_{\mathsf{zk}}ϵzk​ constants such that ϵs+ϵzk<1\epsilon_\mathsf{s} + \epsilon_{\mathsf{zk}} < 1ϵs​+ϵzk​<1) can be amplified to make their errors negligible, but needed to assume the existence of one-way functions. Our results can be used to remove the additional one-way function assumption and obtain NIZK amplification theorems that are (almost) unconditional; only requiring the mild worst-case assumption that if NP⊆ioP/poly\mathsf{NP} \subseteq \mathsf{ioP/poly}NP⊆ioP/poly, then NP⊆BPP\mathsf{NP} \subseteq \mathsf{BPP}NP⊆BPP.
