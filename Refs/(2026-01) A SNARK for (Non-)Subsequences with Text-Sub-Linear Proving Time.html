Abstract

A keyword s\mathbf{s}s is a subsequence of a text t\mathbf{t}t if s\mathbf{s}s can be obtained by deleting some characters from t\mathbf{t}t; otherwise, s\mathbf{s}s is a non-subsequence of t\mathbf{t}t. (Non-)subsequence relationships arise in various fields, including genetic analysis, blockchains, and natural language processing. Recently, Ling et al. (SCN 2024) proposed a succinct argument for non-subsequences based on multivariate sumcheck (Lund et al., FOCS 1990) whose prover's running time is at least O(n+N+∣Σ∣)\mathcal{O}(n + N + |\Sigma|)O(n+N+∣Σ∣), where nnn and NNN are respectively the lengths of strings s\mathbf{s}s and t\mathbf{t}t, and Σ\SigmaΣ is the alphabet over which s\mathbf{s}s and t\mathbf{t}t are defined. As shown in their work, proving non-subsequence relationships is non-trivial since one needs to decompose such an argument into smaller components for sumcheck, permutation, and lookup.

We propose a subsequence scheme that separates proving (non-)subsequences into the following two phases: (i) a preprocessing phase and (ii) a (non-)subsequence proving phase, assuming n≪Nn \ll Nn≪N (i.e., ∣s∣≪∣t∣|\mathbf{s}| \ll |\mathbf{t}|∣s∣≪∣t∣). Specifically, we can generate a one-time preprocessing proof with inputs t\mathbf{t}t and Σ\SigmaΣ, without any knowledge of s\mathbf{s}s. When s\mathbf{s}s is known, we can determine whether s\mathbf{s}s is a subsequence of t\mathbf{t}t and prove the corresponding statement. Employing cached quotients (IACR ePrint 2022/1763), we achieve a running time quasi-linear in N+∣Σ∣N + |\Sigma|N+∣Σ∣ for preprocessing, while the running time of proving a (non-)subsequence relationship is O(n\log2(N+∣Σ∣))\mathcal{O}(n \log_2 (N + |\Sigma|))O(nlog2​(N+∣Σ∣)) for each query s\mathbf{s}s. Since n≪Nn \ll Nn≪N and \log2(N+∣Σ∣)\log_2(N + |\Sigma|)log2​(N+∣Σ∣) grows sub-linearly with the text size, this saves the prover's running time, assuming a preprocessing depending only on t\mathbf{t}t is computed in advance. Hence, we achieve a \textit{text-sub-linear} proving time.
