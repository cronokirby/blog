(2025-06) A search to distinguish reduction for the isomorphism problem on direct sum lattices

  • [[Daniël van Gent]]
  • [[Wessel van Woerden]]
2025-06-27
  • #cryptography
  • #paper

Abstract

At Eurocrypt 2003, Szydlo presented a search to distinguish reduction for the Lattice Isomorphism Problem (LIP) on the integer lattice Zn\mathbb{Z}^nZn. Here the search problem asks to find an isometry between Zn\mathbb{Z}^nZn and an isomorphic lattice, while the distinguish variant asks to distinguish between a list of auxiliary lattices related to Zn\mathbb{Z}^nZn.

In this work we generalize Szydlo's search to distinguish reduction in two ways. Firstly, we generalize the reduction to any lattice isomorphic to Γn\Gamma^nΓn, where Γ\GammaΓ is a fixed base lattice. Secondly, we allow Γ\GammaΓ to be a module lattice over any number field. Assuming the base lattice Γ\GammaΓ and the number field KKK are fixed, our reduction is polynomial in nnn.

As a special case we consider the module lattice OK2\mathcal{O}_K^2OK2​ used in the module-LIP based signature scheme HAWK, and we show that one can solve the search problem, leading to a full key recovery, with less than 2d22d^22d2 distinguishing calls on two lattices each, where ddd is the degree of the power-of-two cyclotomic number field and OK\mathcal{O}_KOK​ its ring of integers.
