Abstract

Consider the problem of merging inside a garbled circuit (GC) two arrays of www-bit elements, yielding a single length-nnn array. This garbled merge problem is core to garbled random access memory (GRAM), a technique that enables efficient garbling of general-purpose programs. We present a novel symmetric-key-based garbled merge that achieves a garbling size of (w+1)⋅n⋅λ(w + 1) \cdot n \cdot \lambda(w+1)⋅n⋅λ bits, providing both asymptotic and concrete improvements over the state of the art. By applying our garbled merge, we obtain a symmetric-key GRAM of size O(n\lg3n⋅λ)⋅ω(1)O(n \lg^3 n \cdot \lambda) \cdot \omega(1)O(nlg3n⋅λ)⋅ω(1) for a word RAM program that manipulates words of size Θ(\lgn)\Theta(\lg n)Θ(lgn) bits and halts within nnn steps, improving over the previous best result (Heath et al., CRYPTO'23) by an O(\lg\lgn)O(\lg \lg n)O(lglgn) factor. This communication cost was previously only achieved under the public-key-style DDH assumption (Gu et al., CRYPTO'25). We implement our construction, and our evaluation shows that our garbled merge reduces the communication cost over the DDH-based merge by about 3×3\times3×.
