(2026-02) Beyond the Linear Barrier; Secret Sharing for Evolving (Weighted) Threshold Access Structures with Poly-logarithmic Share Size

Abstract

Evolving secret sharing allows a dealer to share a secret message between a growing number of nnn parties. Crucially, the dealer does not know an upper bound on nnn, neither it knows the access structure before party nnn arrives; furthermore, the dealer is not allowed to update the shares of old parties. We construct new secret sharing schemes for so-called evolving (weighted) threshold access, in which the arrival of party nnn determines the number of parties tn≤nt_n \le ntn​≤n that are required in order to reconstruct the secret. We also consider the more general case in which party nnn has associated a weight wnw_nwn​ with logarithmic size, and the authorized subsets of parties are those for which the sum of the corresponding weights exceeds the current threshold tnt_ntn​. In particular, we obtain:

  • A secret sharing scheme for evolving threshold access structures with adaptive privacy in the plain model, and with share size poly(λ,\logn)\mathsf{poly}(\lambda,\log n)poly(λ,logn). This construction requires one-way functions (OWFs) and indistinguishability obfuscation (iO) for Turing machines.
  • A secret sharing scheme for evolving weighted threshold access structures with adaptive privacy in the plain model, and with share size poly(λ,\logWn)\mathsf{poly}(\lambda,\log W_n)poly(λ,logWn​) where WnW_nWn​ is the sum of the weights up to party nnn. This construction requires OWFs and iO for Turing machines, and additionally assumes that the weights are fixed (i.e., cannot change over time).
  • A secret sharing scheme for evolving weighted threshold access structures with static privacy in the plain model, and with share size poly(λ,\logWn)\mathsf{poly}(\lambda,\log W_n)poly(λ,logWn​). This construction allows the weight of old parties to change over time, but it requires somewhere statistically binding hash functions and achieves only static privacy. Previous constructions of secret sharing schemes for evolving (weighted) threshold access structures achieved (much worse) share sizes linear in WnW_nWn​ (and in the security parameter) and, when considering adaptive privacy, they require the random oracle model.
