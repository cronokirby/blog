Abstract

DAG-based Byzantine fault-tolerant (BFT) consensus protocols achieve high throughput by allowing many validators to propose concurrently, but scaling them to large committees remains challenging. In a committee of nnn validators, up to fff of which may be Byzantine (n=3f+1n = 3f{+}1n=3f+1), dense round-based DAG designs require each block to reference at least 2f+12f{+}12f+1 blocks from the previous round. This yields O(n)O(n)O(n) metadata per block, O(n2)O(n^2)O(n2) metadata per round, and O(n3)O(n^3)O(n3) metadata bytes transmitted per round under all-to-all dissemination, increasing bandwidth and processing costs and making metadata, rather than payload, the latency bottleneck.

We present Bluestreak, a sparse uncertified DAG BFT consensus protocol that keeps non-leader blocks constant-size (in nnn) and concentrates committee-scale ancestry in a single leader block per round, yielding constant \emph{average} metadata per block as committees grow. Bluestreak combines this sparse block format with a new leader commit rule co-designed for the sparse DAG and a new pull-based pacemaker, and we prove safety and liveness under partial synchrony using only collision-resistant hashes and standard digital signatures.

We implement and evaluate Bluestreak under wide-area latency spanning ten geo-distributed regions. Bluestreak scales from 10 to 400 validators on commodity 4-vCPU instances with sub-second WAN latency throughout (≈470{\approx}\,470≈470 ms at n=10n{=}10n=10, ≈720{\approx}\,720≈720 ms at n=400n{=}400n=400), keeping average per-block metadata constant at ≈320{\approx}\,320≈320 bytes. At n=120n{=}120n=120, Bluestreak sustains ≈220{\approx}\,220≈220k tx/s with LSM-tree storage and ≈400{\approx}\,400≈400k tx/s with WAL-based storage, both at sub-second latency.
