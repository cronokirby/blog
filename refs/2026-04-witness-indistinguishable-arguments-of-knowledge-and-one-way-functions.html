Abstract

In this paper we study the cryptographic complexity of non-trivial witness-indistinguishable (WI) arguments of knowledge. We establish that:

  • Assuming that NP⊈P/polyNP\not\subseteq P/polyNP⊆P/poly, the existence of a constant-round computational WI argument of knowledge for NPNPNP implies that (infinitely-often) auxiliary-input one-way functions exist.
  • Assuming that NP⊈PSam/poly NP\nsubseteq P^{Sam}/polyNP⊈PSam/poly, there is no black-box construction of a constant-round (unbounded-verifier) statistical WI argument of knowledge from one-way permutations. Here, SamSamSam is the collision finder oracle of Haitner, Hoch, Reingold, and Segev [FOCS '07].

Moreover, we identify a natural class of knowledge extractors for which stronger versions of the above implications hold (e.g., even if the protocols have many rounds).
