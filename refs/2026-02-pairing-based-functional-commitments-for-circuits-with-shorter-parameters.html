(2026-02) Pairing-based Functional Commitments for Circuits with Shorter Parameters

  • [[David Balbás]]
  • [[Dario Fiore]]
  • [[Russell W. F. Lai]]
2026-02-24
  • #cryptography

Abstract

Functional commitments (FCs) enable a prover to commit to a message and later produce a succinct proof of its image under any given admissible function. Unlike succinct non-interactive arguments (SNARGs), secure FCs can be realised under falsifiable assumptions in the standard model, making them attractive alternatives when fully algebraic constructions are desired. All known algebraic constructions of FC are either lattice-based or pairing-based. While a lattice-based FC for circuits with almost optimal complexities has been recently achieved [Wee, CRYPTO'25], state-of-the-art pairing-based FCs for bounded-width www [Balbás-Catalano-Fiore-Lai (BCFL), TCC'23] and bounded-size sss circuits [Wee-Wu, EUROCRYPT'24] require prohibitive public parameter sizes O(λw5)O(\lambda w^5)O(λw5) and O(λs5)O(\lambda s^5)O(λs5), respectively.

In this work, we present a new algebraic pairing-based FC which achieves O(λw3)O(\lambda w^3)O(λw3) public parameter size for bounded-width www and unbounded depth ddd circuits. The construction preserves all nice properties of BCFL: O(λ)O(\lambda)O(λ) commitment size, O(λd2)O(\lambda d^2)O(λd2) proof size, additive homomorphism, efficient verification, and chainability. For bounded-size sss circuits, we alternatively obtain O(λs3)O(\lambda s^3)O(λs3) public parameters with O(λd)O(\lambda d)O(λd) proofs. At the core of our scheme lies a new chainable FC for quadratic functions with commitments computed with respect to a power basis, as well as techniques for switching between different commitment bases.
