Abstract

In this work we study quantitative existence results for genus-222 curves over Q\mathbb{Q}Q whose Jacobians have Mordell-Weil rank at least 111 or 222, ordering the curves by the naive height of their integral Weierstrass models. We use geometric techniques to show that asymptotically the Jacobians of almost all integral models with two rational points at infinity have rank r≥1r \geq 1r≥1. We further present a large explicit subfamily where Jacobians have ranks r≥2r \geq 2r≥2, yielding an unconditional logarithmic density of at least 5/75/75/7. Independently, we give a construction of genus-222 curves with split Jacobian and rank 222, producing a subfamily of logarithmic density at least 2/21 2/212/21. Finally, we analyze quadratic and biquadratic twist families in the split-Jacobian setting, obtaining a positive proportion of rank-222 twists. These results have implications for Regev's quantum algorithm in hyperelliptic curve cryptography.
