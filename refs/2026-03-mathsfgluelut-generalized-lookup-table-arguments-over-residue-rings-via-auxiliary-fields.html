Abstract

Lookup Table (LUT) arguments are a central efficiency primitive in modern SNARKs, and existing high-performance constructions are largely tailored to large fields. Meanwhile, an increasingly important class of applications is natively ring-based, with arithmetic carried out over residue rings ZQ:=Z/QZ\mathbb{Z}_Q:=\mathbb{Z}/Q\mathbb{Z}ZQ​:=Z/QZ. We find that naively extending field-based lookup table techniques to rings faces fundamental obstacles, which can lead to unsoundness, limited applicability, or poor efficiency.

We introduce GlueLUT\mathsf{GlueLUT}GlueLUT, a general framework for constructing LUT arguments over arbitrary residue ring ZQ\mathbb{Z}_QZQ​ that supports arbitrary tables. Our main technical tool is a new primitive called Cross-Modulus Consistency (CMC) PIOP, proves that two witnesses defined over coprime moduli share the same underlying integer in the canonical range. Using our CMC PIOP as a glue, we perform the lookups over an auxiliary field FP\mathbb{F}_PFP​ (for a prime P>QP>QP>Q) and then certify the consistency between the witness over ZQ\mathbb{Z}_QZQ​ and the witness over FP\mathbb{F}_PFP​, thereby avoiding the obstacles of constructing LUT arguments directly over rings. We further provide two optimized instantiations, GlueLUT\mathsf{GlueLUT}GlueLUT-v1\mathsf{v1}v1 for Q=pqQ=pqQ=pq and GlueLUT\mathsf{GlueLUT}GlueLUT-v2\mathsf{v2}v2 for Q=pkQ=p^kQ=pk, capturing common modulus families in practice. Finally, we implement GlueLUT\mathsf{GlueLUT}GlueLUT-v1\mathsf{v1}v1 and GlueLUT\mathsf{GlueLUT}GlueLUT-v2\mathsf{v2}v2 as stand-alone PIOPs and report prototype results that corroborate our theoretical efficiency.
