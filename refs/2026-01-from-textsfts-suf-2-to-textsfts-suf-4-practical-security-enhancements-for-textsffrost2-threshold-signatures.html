Abstract

Threshold signature schemes play a vital role in securing digital assets within blockchain and distributed systems. FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2 stands out as a practical threshold Schnorr signature scheme, noted for its efficiency and compatibility with standard verification processes. However, under the one-more discrete logarithm assumption, with static corruption and centralized key generation settings, FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2 has been shown by Bellare et al. (in CRYPTO 2022) to achieve only TS-SUF-2\textsf{TS-SUF-2}TS-SUF-2 security, which is a consequence of its vulnerability to TS-UF-3\textsf{TS-UF-3}TS-UF-3 attacks.

In this paper, we address this security limitation by presenting two enhanced variants of FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2: FROST2+\textsf{FROST2}\texttt{+}FROST2+ and $\textsf{FROST2}\texttt{#}$, both achieving the TS-SUF-4\textsf{TS-SUF-4}TS-SUF-4 security level under the same computational assumptions as the original FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2. The first variant, FROST2+\textsf{FROST2}\texttt{+}FROST2+, strengthens FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2 by integrating additional pre-processing token verifications that help mitigate TS-UF-3\textsf{TS-UF-3}TS-UF-3 and TS-UF-4\textsf{TS-UF-4}TS-UF-4 vulnerabilities while maintaining practical efficiency. We show that FROST2+\textsf{FROST2}\texttt{+}FROST2+ can achieve TS-SUF-4\textsf{TS-SUF-4}TS-SUF-4 security not only under the same conditions as the original FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2 analysis, but also when initialized with a distributed key generation protocol such as PedPoP\textsf{PedPoP}PedPoP. Building on these improvements, we identify optimization opportunities that lead to our second variant, $\textsf{FROST2}\texttt{#}$, which achieves TS-SUF-4\textsf{TS-SUF-4}TS-SUF-4 security with enhanced computational efficiency by eliminating redundant calculations. Our benchmark shows that the performance of FROST2+\textsf{FROST2}\texttt{+}FROST2+ is comparable to FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2 while $\textsf{FROST2}\texttt{#}$ is at least 3 times faster than FROST2\textsf{FROST2}FROST2.
