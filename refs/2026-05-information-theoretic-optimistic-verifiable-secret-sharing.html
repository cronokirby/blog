Abstract

Verifiable secret sharing (VSS) is a fundamental primitive for secure computation and its round complexity has been well studied. The works of Gennaro et al. [STOC'01] and Fitzi et al. [TCC'06] settled the landscape in the perfect-security setting, showing that for the optimal corruption threshold t<n/3t<n/3t<n/3, the exact round complexity is three, and for the sub-optimal corruption threshold t<n/4t<n/4t<n/4 it is two rounds. Similarly, Patra et al. [CRYPTO'09] and Kumaresan et al. [ASIACRYPT'10] settled the landscape in the statistical setting, showing that for t<n/2t<n/2t<n/2 (resp. t<n/3t<n/3t<n/3), the exact round complexity is three (resp. two).

Current protocols with optimal resilience incur three rounds even when the actual number of corruptions fff is sub-optimal. Fix corruption threshold parameters 0≤k≤t0\le k \le t0≤k≤t. We ask whether it is possible to obtain a VSS protocol that incurs two rounds when f≤kf\le kf≤k, and three rounds when k<f≤tk<f\le tk<f≤t. We show matching feasibility and impossibility results demonstrating that this is possible if and only if 3t+k<n3t+k < n3t+k<n for perfect security, and 2t+k<n2t+k < n2t+k<n for statistical security.
