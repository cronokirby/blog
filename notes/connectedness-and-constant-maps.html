Connectedness and Constant Maps

The usual definition of a connected space is something like:

A topological space XXX is connected if it cannot be written as the disjoint union of open sets.

In more formal terms, if there do not exist U,VU, VU,V open and nonempty, with U∩V=∅U \cap V = \emptyU∩V=∅, and U∪V=XU \cup V = XU∪V=X, then XXX is connected.

We call such U,VU, VU,V a separation of XXX.

A slightly more useful definition is:

A topological space XXX is connected if and only if every continuous map to {0,1}\{0, 1\}{0,1} (with the discrete topology) is constant.

One way of thinking about this, is that you can only assign different colors to parts of a space (in a continuous way) if that space is disconnected.
